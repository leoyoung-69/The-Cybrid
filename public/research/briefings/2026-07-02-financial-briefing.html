Scarcity vs. Abundance

BlackRock's latest macro commentary frames the central investment tension of the current cycle as 'scarcity vs. abundance' — a structural dichotomy between constrained physical supply (energy, infrastructure, commodities) and abundant capital chasing AI and digital infrastructure. The piece argues that this divergence creates both sector-level dislocations and portfolio diversification opportunities that traditional asset allocation frameworks may miss.

Key takeaways
  • Structural scarcity in energy and physical infrastructure vs. abundance in AI capital spending creates divergent risk/return profiles across sectors.
  • Investors need to reassess diversification assumptions as the traditional stock-bond correlation continues to break down.
  • BlackRock maintains an overweight to real assets and inflation-linked instruments in this environment.
